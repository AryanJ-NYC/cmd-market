## Product Overview
**One-liner:** CMD Market is a Scarce City marketplace that helps OpenClaw-native sellers turn item photos into structured listings with less manual work.

**What it does:** Sellers create browser-backed seller workspaces, connect OpenClaw when they are ready, and use agent-assisted flows to draft, enrich, and publish listings. Buyers can discover CMD Market inventory through both the web app and their own agents.

**Product category:** Agent-first marketplace, seller listing workflow, peer-to-peer marketplace

**Product type:** Marketplace with agent integrations

**Business model:** Business model and pricing are not locked in publicly in this repo yet. Do not invent pricing, fees, subscription language, or revenue claims.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Manual marketplace listing is repetitive, slow, and mentally draining.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Manual listing forces sellers to repeat titles, descriptions, specifics, pricing, and media steps by hand.
- Dashboard-first cross-listing tools still ask sellers to leave their existing workflow and manage another product.
- Generic marketplaces are not designed around agent-readable inventory or agent-mediated discovery.

**What it costs them:** Lost time, slower listing throughput, more decision fatigue, and lower willingness to keep selling consistently

**Emotional tension:** Sellers want speed and leverage, but they worry about losing control, publishing inaccurate details, or adding yet another setup burden

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Cross-listing tools such as Crosslist, Vendoo, List Perfectly, and Nifty. They solve similar workflow pain, but they are dashboard-first and can add another subscription, another interface, and more context switching.

**Secondary:** Manual listing on marketplaces like eBay, Mercari, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and category-specific destinations. This is the default behavior sellers already know, but it is repetitive and slow.

**Indirect:** Doing nothing, selling locally with minimal structure, or relying on informal channels. This keeps setup low but limits reach, structure, and repeatability.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- OpenClaw-native workflow
- Structured inventory built for both people and agents
- Browser-first seller setup with agent assistance layered in after trust is established
- Shared marketplace contract for human web flows and agent flows

**How we do it differently:** CMD Market starts with a browser-backed seller workspace, then lets sellers connect OpenClaw to assist with drafts, media, and publishing flows instead of forcing everything through a standalone dashboard.

**Why that is better:** Sellers get less repetitive work without giving up control. Listings become cleaner and easier for both people and agent-powered buyers to understand.

**Why customers choose us:** If they already use OpenClaw or are comfortable selling through agents, CMD Market is the most natural path to faster listing with cleaner inventory structure.

**Anti-persona:** People who want zero behavior change, zero setup, and no agent involvement today; enterprise merchants looking for ERP-grade multichannel tooling; users looking for fully managed consignment; buyers looking for broad retail price-comparison

## Switching Dynamics
